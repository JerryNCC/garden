### <center>Countercultural Reconciliation  </center>

> [!question]+ Review
>

> [!info]- ğŸ™ï¸Audio: [None]()  


Philemon is a short letter from Paul to a man named Philemon. Philemon appears to have been a wealthy man who owned a slave named Onesimus. Onesimus had run away from Philemon and may have stolen from him in the process. But in the midst of his flight, Onesimus met Paul. He repented of his sin, put his faith in Jesus Christ, and became a dear friend to Paul while Paul was imprisoned in Rome.

This created an interesting situation. Onesimus had wronged his earthly master, yet now he was a Christian, sharing faith in Christ with his former owner. Paul steps into this situation with wise counsel. He tells Onesimus that he needs to make things right with Philemon. But Paul also appeals to Philemon. Reconciliation with God should flow into reconciliation with Godâ€™s people. That is the heart of Paulâ€™s message in the letter that Onesimus carries to his master.

Paul does not command Philemon to forgive Onesimus, but he strongly urges him to do so on the basis of the gospel. He asks Philemon to receive Onesimus â€œno longer as a slave, but better than a slave, as a beloved brother â€¦ both in the flesh and in the Lordâ€ (v.16). Paul promises to cover any debt Onesimus owes and encourages Philemon to take him in as an equal. Paul is gentle but firm in his appeal.

Why does Paul press this point? Such a response would have been countercultural. Runaway slaves could be legally punished, even killed. But Paul shows that people of the gospel act differently. Onesimusâ€™s reconciliation with God through Christ calls for forgiveness and restoration with Philemon. Philemon is to show grace to his brother in Christ, just as Christ has shown grace to him. Their relationship is changed forever; they are brothers in Christ, saved by the same Savior.

> [!important]+ Reflect and Respond
Do you hold anger toward someone, or does someone hold something against you? If you are reconciled to God through repentance and faith in Christ, you are called to be reconciled with your brothers and sisters in Christ. Ask God today to give you courage to take the first step toward confession, forgiveness, and reconciliation.
